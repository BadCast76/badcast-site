What to bring

Sunblock (non-spray), sunglasses, and a water bottle. Feel free to bring food and drinks. Alcohol is fine in moderation — skip the hard liquor and glass bottles. Dress in layers; mornings on the water are cool even in summer.

What We Target

The New Meadows watershed and surrounding Casco Bay waters hold a strong mix of species through the season.

  • Striped Bass — the main event. Drew knows where they run.
  • Bluefish — hard fighters, especially on light tackle and the fly.
  • Atlantic Mackerel — great on the fly, great for beginners.
  • Pollock — a local staple and a strong fighter near structure.
  • Cod & Haddock — bottom fishing when conditions are right.
  • Blue Shark — licensed and available with advance planning.
  • Tuna — licensed and available. Some prep required. Ask Drew.

Your Captain

Captain Drew Renda

Drew has been fishing the New Meadows watershed for 20 years. He knows the tides, the ledges, and the drifts, and he loves sharing that knowledge with people who want to learn.

Whether you've never held a fly rod or you've been casting for decades, Drew meets you where you are. He's patient, communicative, and focused on making sure you have a great day on the water.

F/V Bad Cast is a 21-foot Grady-White rigged for both fly fishing and spin casting. Safety gear is always on board. The boat is licensed for shark and tuna if you want to push further out.

He doesn't wait with bait. He slings it.
